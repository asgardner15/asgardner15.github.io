Basic Setup

American Mahjongg is usually played with four players seated around a table. Each player uses a rack to hold their tiles so the other players cannot see them. The game uses tiles, racks, dice, and the current National Mah Jongg League card.

Before play begins, the tiles are shuffled and arranged into walls. The dealer, known as East, starts the round. Players receive their tiles, organize them on their racks, and begin looking for possible hands that match the card.

After the tiles are dealt, players complete a passing step called the Charleston. During the Charleston, players pass unwanted tiles to other players to improve their hands before regular play begins.

Once the Charleston is complete, East discards the first tile. Players then take turns drawing, racking, and discarding tiles while working toward a winning hand.

Main Tile Types

American Mahjongg uses three main numbered tile suits that beginners should learn first.

  • Dots: Circular symbols numbered 1 through 9
  • Bams: Bamboo symbols numbered 1 through 9
  • Cracks: Chinese character tiles numbered 1 through 9

In addition to the three numbered suits, the game also includes honor tiles and special tiles:

  • Winds: North, South, East, and West tiles
  • Dragons: Special honor tiles
  • Jokers: Wild cards used in many tile combinations

The Annual Card

American Mahjongg uses a yearly card published by the National Mah Jongg League. The card lists the official winning hands players try to complete during the game.

Beginners often start by learning how to read the card and recognize tile patterns before focusing on advanced strategy.

What to Do on Your Turn

During the game, players take turns drawing a tile, placing it on their rack, and discarding one unwanted tile. Players continue this process while working toward a matching hand from the card.

If another player discards a tile you need for a matching hand, you may be able to call that tile. A player wins by completing a valid hand from the card and declaring Mahjongg.

Simple Overview of a Round

  1. Tiles are shuffled and walls are built.
  2. Each player receives 13 tiles for their rack, with the dealer receiving 14.
  3. Players draw and discard tiles one at a time, with the dealer beginning the round by discarding a tile.
  4. Players try to complete one of the official hands listed on the NMJL card.
  5. The first player to complete a valid hand wins the round.

Beginner Advice

Learning Mahjongg takes practice. Most new players improve by focusing on recognizing tile groups, understanding the card, and playing regularly with experienced players.
